Directory of Open Access Books (DOAB)

DOAB is a community-driven discovery service that indexes and provides access to over 101,500 scholarly, peer-reviewed open access books. It helps users find trusted open access book publishers and provides free access to academic publications across various subjects and languages. All DOAB services are free of charge and all data is freely available for librarians, researchers, and publishers.

Visit DOAB →

GhaLII - Ghana Legal Information Institute

GhaLII is a non-profit organisation based in Accra that digitises and publishes the law of Ghana for free access to all, in partnership with the Judicial Service of Ghana. GhaLII provides free access to digital parliamentary, legislative, and judicial information from Ghana and ECOWAS, including judgments from superior and lower courts, legislation, government gazettes, and African Union and ECOWAS legal materials. The platform promotes access to legal information as a fundamental part of the rule of law, making Ghana's legal system more accessible to researchers, legal professionals, and the general public.

Visit GhaLII →

Koha OPAC - Baobab eBooks

Koha OPAC (Online Public Access Catalogue) is an open-source integrated library system that provides access to library collections and resources. The Baobab eBooks catalog allows users to search and discover digital books and resources available through the library system. Access the online catalog to browse collections, check availability, and explore digital resources.

Visit Koha OPAC →

PDF Drive

PDF Drive is a comprehensive search engine for PDF files, offering access to millions of free PDF documents, books, and research papers. Users can search, preview, and download PDF files across various categories including academic texts, research papers, technical manuals, and general interest books. A valuable resource for finding and accessing PDF documents for research and learning purposes.

Visit PDF Drive →

Project Gutenberg

Project Gutenberg is a library of over 75,000 free eBooks, offering access to the world's great literature with a focus on older works for which U.S. copyright has expired. This volunteer-driven project has been pioneering free eBooks since 1971, making classic literature and historical texts freely available to readers worldwide. All eBooks are completely free with no fees, no registration required, and no apps needed - just your regular web browser or eBook reader.
